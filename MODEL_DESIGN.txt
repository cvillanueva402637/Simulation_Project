================================================================================
           MODEL DESIGN — KIDNEY TRANSPLANT LOGISTICS &
              PERISHABLE QUEUEING SIMULATION
================================================================================
           Arena-Style Discrete-Event Simulation (Python / SimPy)
================================================================================


1. OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The simulation model represents a kidney transplant system where two types of
entities — donor kidneys and waitlisted patients — flow through a network of
processes that mirror real-world organ procurement, matching, testing,
transportation, and surgical transplantation. The distinguishing feature of this
model is that kidneys are perishable entities subject to a cold ischemia clock
that begins at harvest and imposes a hard 36-hour viability deadline with
continuous exponential quality decay.

The model is built using an Arena-equivalent discrete-event simulation
framework (SimPy) and is structured entirely around Arena's core module types:
Create, Assign, Decide, Process (Seize-Delay-Release), Hold/Queue, and Dispose.


2. ENTITIES
================================================================================

The model has two entity types, each with a set of Arena-style attributes:

  ENTITY 1 — KidneyOrgan (Perishable)
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  An organ entity that enters the system upon donor procurement. Each kidney
  carries the following attributes:
    • Entity ID          — Unique serial number
    • Blood Type         — ABO group (O, A, B, AB), sampled from US population
                           distribution (O: 44%, A: 42%, B: 10%, AB: 4%)
    • HLA Profile        — 6-antigen tissue typing (each antigen randomly 1–20)
    • Donor Type         — "Deceased" or "Living"
    • Harvest Time       — Simulation clock time when the organ was procured
    • Quality Score      — Starts at 1.0 and decays via Q(t) = e^(-0.05·t)
    • Max Viable Time    — 36 hours (cold ischemia limit)
    • Transport Zone     — Local, Regional, or National
    • Status             — Tracks position in flowchart (Harvested, In Pool,
                           Matched, Crossmatch Testing, In Transport, In Surgery,
                           Transplanted, Expired, Rejected)

  ENTITY 2 — Patient (Waitlisted)
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  A patient entity that enters the transplant waitlist. Attributes include:
    • Entity ID          — Unique serial number
    • Blood Type         — ABO group (same distribution as kidneys)
    • HLA Profile        — 6-antigen tissue typing
    • Urgency Level      — UNOS-style: Critical (10%), High (25%),
                           Medium (40%), Low (25%)
    • Time on Waitlist   — Clock time when patient joined the list
    • Age                — Uniformly sampled between 18–75 years
    • PRA                — Panel Reactive Antibody percentage (0–100%);
                           higher values indicate sensitized patients who are
                           harder to match
    • Priority Score     — Composite: (Urgency Weight × 10) + (Wait Days × 0.5)
                           + (PRA/100 × 5)
    • Status             — Waiting, Matched, Transplanted, Died Waiting,
                           Transferred


3. MODULES USED
================================================================================

The simulation model is designed using the following Arena-equivalent modules:


  MODULE 1 — CREATE: Deceased Donor Kidney Arrivals
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Generates kidney organ entities representing deceased-donor procurement events.
  Inter-arrival times follow an Exponential distribution with a rate of 1/8 per
  hour (approximately 3 kidneys per day). Upon creation, each kidney entity is
  assigned randomized blood type, HLA profile, and donor type attributes. The
  cold ischemia clock starts immediately at creation time.

  MODULE 2 — CREATE: Patient Arrivals to Waitlist
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Generates patient entities who need a kidney transplant. Inter-arrival times
  follow an Exponential distribution with a rate of 1/4 per hour (approximately
  6 patients per day). Each patient is assigned a randomized blood type, HLA
  profile, urgency level, age, and PRA sensitization score. Upon creation,
  the patient entity enters the waitlist queue and also triggers a check of
  the organ pool to see if any currently available kidney is compatible.

  MODULE 3 — CREATE: Living Donor Kidney Arrivals
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Generates pre-scheduled living donor kidney entities at a lower rate
  (Exponential, rate 1/48 per hour, approximately 0.5 per day). Living donor
  kidneys arrive with a guaranteed-compatible recipient, so they bypass the
  matching queue entirely and proceed directly to crossmatch testing and
  surgery.

  MODULE 4 — ASSIGN: Kidney Attributes
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Upon creation, each kidney entity is assigned:
    • Blood type (sampled from ABO distribution)
    • HLA antigen profile (6 random antigens)
    • Transport distance zone — Local (40%), Regional (35%), National (25%)
  The transport zone determines which triangular distribution governs the
  later transport delay time.

  MODULE 5 — ASSIGN: Patient Attributes
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Upon creation, each patient entity is assigned:
    • Blood type (sampled from ABO distribution)
    • HLA antigen profile (6 random antigens)
    • Urgency level (Critical / High / Medium / Low with defined weights)
    • Age (Uniform 18–75)
    • PRA sensitization score (Uniform 0–100%)

  MODULE 6 — DECIDE: Organ-to-Patient Matching (UNOS-Style Allocation)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  This is the core decision logic that determines whether a compatible patient
  exists for an arriving kidney. The matching algorithm applies three filters
  in sequence:

    Step 1 — ABO Blood Type Compatibility:
             The organ's blood type must be compatible with the patient per
             the standard compatibility matrix (e.g., Type O kidneys can go
             to O, A, B, or AB recipients; Type AB can only go to AB).

    Step 2 — HLA Antigen Matching:
             The number of matching HLA antigens (out of 6) is counted.
             Higher matches are preferred but all levels are accepted.

    Step 3 — Virtual Crossmatch (PRA-based):
             Patients with PRA > 80% have a probability of failing the
             virtual crossmatch equal to PRA/200, which may eliminate
             them from candidacy.

  Among all passing candidates, a composite priority score is computed:
    Score = (Urgency Weight × 10) + (Wait Time in Days × 0.5) + (HLA Matches × 2)
  The patient with the highest score is selected.

  If no compatible patient is found, the kidney entity is routed to the
  Organ Pool (Hold module) to wait. If no match is found before the cold
  ischemia clock expires, the organ is disposed as expired.

  MODULE 7 — HOLD / QUEUE: Patient Waitlist (Priority Queue)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Patients who have not yet been matched to a kidney wait in a priority queue.
  When a new kidney arrives, the Decide module scans this queue for the
  highest-priority compatible patient. When a new patient arrives, the Decide
  module also scans the Organ Pool for any available compatible kidney. This
  two-way matching prevents both organs and patients from waiting unnecessarily.

  MODULE 8 — HOLD / QUEUE: Organ Pool
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Kidneys that have no immediate match upon arrival enter the organ pool.
  This is a holding area where organs wait while their cold ischemia clock
  continues to tick. An hourly expiry checker removes any organ whose elapsed
  time since harvest exceeds 36 hours, routing it to the Dispose (Expired)
  module. When a new patient joins the waitlist, pooled organs are re-evaluated
  for potential matches.

  MODULE 9 — PROCESS: Crossmatch Testing (Seize Lab → Delay → Release Lab)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Once a kidney-patient pair is matched, the kidney entity enters the
  crossmatch lab process:
    • Seize:   One of 2 crossmatch laboratory resources
    • Delay:   Triangular(1.0, 2.0, 4.0) hours — physical crossmatch test
    • Release: The lab resource is freed
  If the lab is busy, the entity waits in the crossmatch queue. After testing,
  a Decide sub-module determines the crossmatch result: the failure probability
  is 5% baseline plus an additional PRA/500 for sensitized patients. A positive
  crossmatch (failure) routes the kidney to Dispose.

  MODULE 10 — PROCESS: Organ Transport (Seize Vehicle → Delay → Release Vehicle)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  After passing the crossmatch, the kidney is transported to the surgical center:
    • Seize:   One of 3 transport vehicle resources (helicopter/van)
    • Delay:   Depends on the transport zone assigned earlier:
               - Local:    Triangular(0.5, 1.0, 2.0) hours
               - Regional: Triangular(1.5, 3.0, 5.0) hours
               - National: Triangular(3.0, 5.0, 8.0) hours
    • Release: The transport vehicle is freed
  After transport, a Decide sub-module checks whether the kidney is still within
  its 36-hour viability window. If cold ischemia time has been exceeded, the
  organ is disposed as expired and the patient returns to the waitlist.

  MODULE 11 — PROCESS: Surgery Preparation (Delay Only)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A simple delay representing pre-operative preparation:
    • Delay: Triangular(0.5, 1.0, 2.0) hours
  No resource is seized for this stage. After preparation, a final viability
  check is performed before proceeding to surgery.

  MODULE 12 — PROCESS: Transplant Surgery (Seize OR + Team → Delay → Release)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The actual kidney transplant operation:
    • Seize:   One of 4 Operating Room resources AND one of 5 Surgical Team
               resources simultaneously (both must be available)
    • Delay:   Triangular(2.0, 3.5, 6.0) hours — surgery duration
    • Release: Both the operating room and surgical team are freed
  If either resource is busy, the entity waits in the surgery queue.

  MODULE 13 — DECIDE: Transplant Outcome (Graft Success vs. Rejection)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  After surgery, the model determines whether the transplant succeeds or fails
  using a probabilistic outcome model:
    Success Probability = 0.95 + (0.005 × HLA Matches) - (0.01 × CIT Hours)
    Bounded to [0.70, 1.00]
  Factors that improve success: higher HLA match count, lower cold ischemia
  time. If the transplant succeeds, both the kidney and patient are disposed
  as a successful transplant. If it fails (graft rejection), the kidney is
  disposed and the patient returns to the waitlist to await another organ.

  MODULE 14 — DECIDE: Patient Attrition (Background Process)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A continuous hourly check on all waitlisted patients. Each hour, every active
  patient faces:
    • Death probability:    0.0001 per hour (removes patient from system)
    • Transfer probability: 0.00005 per hour (patient leaves to another center)
  This models the real-world attrition of patients who die or are transferred
  while waiting for a transplant.

  MODULE 15 — DECIDE: Organ Expiry Checker (Background Process)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A continuous hourly scan of the organ pool. Any kidney whose time since
  harvest exceeds 36 hours is removed from the pool and disposed as expired.
  This enforces the perishability constraint and models real-world organ waste.

  MODULE 16 — DISPOSE: Successful Transplant
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Removes the kidney and patient entities from the system after a successful
  transplant. Records statistics including cold ischemia time at transplant,
  organ quality score, patient wait time, HLA match score, and total system
  time.

  MODULE 17 — DISPOSE: Organ Expired
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Removes kidney entities that exceeded the cold ischemia window. Records
  the cold ischemia time at expiry for waste analysis.

  MODULE 18 — DISPOSE: Crossmatch Failed
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Removes kidney entities that had a positive (incompatible) physical
  crossmatch result, preventing an unsafe transplant.

  MODULE 19 — DISPOSE: Graft Rejection
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Removes the kidney entity after a failed transplant surgery. The matched
  patient is re-activated and returned to the waitlist.

  MODULE 20 — DISPOSE: Patient Died / Transferred
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Removes patient entities who died while waiting or who were transferred
  to another transplant center.


4. RESOURCES
================================================================================

The model uses four shared resources with defined capacities. Entities must
seize these resources in Process modules and release them after the associated
delay completes. If all units of a resource are busy, arriving entities queue:

  ┌───────────────────────┬──────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Resource              │ Capacity │ Used By                                │
  ├───────────────────────┼──────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Operating Rooms       │    4     │ Surgery Process (Seize-Delay-Release)  │
  │ Surgical Teams        │    5     │ Surgery Process (Seize-Delay-Release)  │
  │ Transport Vehicles    │    3     │ Transport Process (Seize-Delay-Release)│
  │ Crossmatch Labs       │    2     │ Crossmatch Process (Seize-Delay-Rel.) │
  └───────────────────────┴──────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Note: The Surgery Process requires BOTH an Operating Room AND a Surgical
  Team simultaneously. The entity will wait until both are available before
  proceeding.


5. FLOW OF ENTITIES
================================================================================

The entity flow is organized into three parallel paths that interact through
the matching system:


  PATH A — Deceased Donor Kidney Flow:
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Create (Kidney) → Assign (Blood Type, HLA, Zone)
       → Decide (Match Found?)
            ├── YES → Process (Crossmatch Lab: Seize-Delay-Release)
            │          → Decide (Crossmatch Pass?)
            │               ├── YES → Process (Transport: Seize-Delay-Release)
            │               │          → Decide (Still Viable?)
            │               │               ├── YES → Process (Surgery Prep: Delay)
            │               │               │          → Decide (Still Viable?)
            │               │               │              ├── YES → Process (Surgery: Seize OR+Team, Delay, Release)
            │               │               │              │          → Decide (Graft Success?)
            │               │               │              │               ├── YES → Dispose (Successful Transplant)
            │               │               │              │               └── NO  → Dispose (Graft Rejection)
            │               │               │              │                         Patient → back to Waitlist
            │               │               │              └── NO  → Dispose (Expired)
            │               │               │                        Patient → back to Waitlist
            │               │               └── NO  → Dispose (Expired)
            │               │                        Patient → back to Waitlist
            │               └── NO  → Dispose (Crossmatch Failed)
            │
            └── NO  → Hold (Organ Pool — wait for patient)
                       → Hourly Check: Decide (Expired?)
                            ├── YES → Dispose (Organ Expired)
                            └── NO  → Continue waiting / re-check for new patients


  PATH B — Patient Flow:
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Create (Patient) → Assign (Blood Type, HLA, Urgency, Age, PRA)
       → Queue (Waitlist — Priority Queue)
       → Hourly Check: Decide (Attrition?)
            ├── Death     → Dispose (Died on Waitlist)
            ├── Transfer  → Dispose (Transferred Out)
            └── Survives  → Continue waiting for organ match
       → [When matched by Path A] → Proceeds through Path A's process chain


  PATH C — Living Donor Flow:
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Create (Living Kidney + Recipient) → Assign (Guaranteed Compatible)
       → Process (Crossmatch Lab: Seize-Delay-Release)
       → Process (Transport: Seize-Delay-Release)
       → Process (Surgery: Seize OR+Team, Delay, Release)
       → Decide (Graft Success?)
            ├── YES → Dispose (Successful Transplant)
            └── NO  → Dispose (Graft Rejection)

  Living donors bypass the matching queue because they arrive with a
  pre-identified compatible recipient.


6. LOGICAL STRUCTURE
================================================================================

The simulation's logical structure follows these key design principles:

  a) TWO-WAY MATCHING TRIGGER
     The matching logic is triggered in two directions:
       • When a new kidney arrives → search the patient waitlist
       • When a new patient arrives → search the organ pool
     This prevents situations where a compatible kidney and patient both
     sit idle without being matched.

  b) PERISHABILITY ENFORCEMENT
     The cold ischemia clock runs continuously from the moment of organ
     harvest. Viability is checked at three critical decision points:
       1. After transport (before surgery prep)
       2. After surgery prep (before entering the OR)
       3. Hourly in the organ pool (for unmatched kidneys)
     If any check fails, the organ is immediately disposed.

  c) QUALITY DECAY MODEL
     Organ quality degrades exponentially: Q(t) = e^(-0.05·t), where t is
     hours since harvest. This quality score directly affects the transplant
     success probability, creating a meaningful trade-off between waiting for
     a better match versus transplanting quickly before quality degrades.

  d) PRIORITY-BASED ALLOCATION
     The matching algorithm uses a composite score to allocate organs fairly:
       Score = (Urgency Weight × 10) + (Wait Days × 0.5) + (HLA Matches × 2)
     This means critically ill patients and those who have waited longest
     receive priority, but a strong HLA match can elevate a lower-priority
     patient.

  e) WARM-UP PERIOD
     The first 30 simulated days (720 hours) serve as a warm-up period.
     All statistics collected during the warm-up are discarded. This ensures
     the system reaches a representative steady state before data collection
     begins, avoiding initialization bias.

  f) MULTIPLE REPLICATIONS WITH DIFFERENT SEEDS
     The model runs multiple independent replications (default: 10), each
     with a different random number seed. This enables the calculation of
     confidence intervals on output metrics, providing statistical rigor
     equivalent to Arena's built-in replication analysis.


7. STATISTICS COLLECTED
================================================================================

The model collects three types of statistics, mirroring Arena's statistical
framework:

  TALLY (Observation-Based) Statistics:
    • Organ Cold Ischemia Time (hours) — time from harvest to transplant
    • Organ Quality at Transplant — quality score at surgery completion
    • Patient Wait Time (hours and days) — time from waitlist entry to match
    • Surgery Duration (hours)
    • Transport Time (hours)
    • Crossmatch Testing Time (hours)
    • Organ Total Time in System (hours)
    • HLA Match Score (0–6) — antigen matches in successful transplants
    • Patient Priority Score at Match

  TIME-PERSISTENT (Time-Weighted) Statistics:
    • Waitlist Length — number of active patients over time
    • Organ Pool Size — number of available organs over time
    • Operating Room Utilization (0–1)
    • Surgical Team Utilization (0–1)
    • Transport Vehicle Utilization (0–1)
    • Crossmatch Lab Utilization (0–1)

  COUNTER Statistics:
    • Total Kidneys Arrived
    • Total Patients Arrived
    • Successful Transplants
    • Failed Transplants (Graft Rejection)
    • Organs Expired (Cold Ischemia Exceeded)
    • Organs with No Compatible Match
    • Patients Died on Waitlist
    • Patients Transferred Out
    • Living Donor Transplants

  DERIVED Metrics:
    • Organ Utilization Rate = Successful Transplants / Total Kidneys × 100%
    • Organ Waste Rate = Organs Expired / Total Kidneys × 100%
    • No-Match Rate = No Compatible Match / Total Kidneys × 100%

9. SIMULATION CONTROL PARAMETERS
================================================================================

  • Simulation Duration:   8,760 hours (365 days / 1 year)
  • Warm-Up Period:        720 hours (30 days)
  • Number of Replications: 10 (each with a unique random seed)
  • Base Random Seed:      42 (seeds: 1042, 2042, 3042, ...)
  • Report Interval:       Every 24 hours (daily snapshots)
